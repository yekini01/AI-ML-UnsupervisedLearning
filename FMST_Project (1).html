Project - Credit Card Customer Segmentation¶

AllLife Credit Card Customer Segmentation¶

Objective:¶

To identify different segments in the existing customer based on their spending patterns as well as past interaction with the bank.

Background¶

AllLife Bank wants to focus on its credit card customer base in the next financial year. They have been advised by their marketing research team, that the penetration in the market can be improved. Based on this input, the Marketing team proposes to run personalised campaigns to target new customers as well as upsell to existing customers. Another insight from the market research was that the customers perceive the support services of the back poorly. Based on this, the Operations team wants to upgrade the service delivery model, to ensure that customers queries are resolved faster. Head of Marketing and Head of Delivery both decide to reach out to the Data Science team for help.

Key Questions¶

  1. How many different segments of customers are there?
  2. How are these segments different from each other?
  3. What are your recommendations to the bank on how to better market to and service these customers?

Data Description¶

Data is of various customers of a bank with their credit limit, the total number of credit cards the customer has, and different channels through which customer has contacted the bank for any queries, different channels include visiting the bank, online and through a call.

  • Customer key - Identifier for the customer
  • Average Credit Limit - Average credit limit across all the credit cards
  • Total credit cards - Total number of credit cards
  • Total visits bank - Total number of bank visits
  • Total visits online - total number of online visits
  • Total calls made - Total number of calls made by the customer

Dataset Information¶

The data for this project is available in file 'Credit Card Customer Data.xlsx'

6. Compare K-means clusters with Hierarchical clusters. (5 marks)¶

K Means clustering needed advance knowledge of no. of clusters,k. In hierarchical clustering one can decide at any number of clusters, based on interpreting the dendrogram. One can use median, centroid or mean as a cluster centre to represent each cluster.Agglomerative methods begin with ‘n’ clusters and sequentially combine similar clusters until only one cluster is obtained. I found the K-means method easier to use and more flexible compared with the hierarchical clustering method. More details as to the differences can be obtained from https://towardsdatascience.com/unsupervised-learning-k-means-vs-hierarchical-clustering-5fe2da7c9554

Key Questions¶

  1. How many different segments of customers are there? The cluster analyses described suggests 3 different customer segments
  1. How are these segments different from each other? These segments differ as follows: First segment visits the bank most often for their transactions. They have relatively few number of credit cards and the low available credit. The second segment are very active online and prefer online transactions. They have the most Total number of credit cards and the highest Average Credit Limit. The third segment of users prefer transactions by Phone calls. They have the least Total number of credit cards and the least Average Credit Limit.
  1. What are your recommendations to the bank on how to better market to and service these customers? The online customer segment appear to be very savvy and focused modern digital marketing in conjunction with social networking can be used to market the bank's products to these category of customers. The in-person segment can be targetted by marketing the products at the bank locations and perhaps understanding the factors preventing these category from online presence. Since this category have few credit cards and lowest average credit limit, this is an opportunity to get good return on marketing investment from these category. The third category or customer segment should be targeted using modern mobile phone marketing campaigns including text. These category also opens up opportunities for the bank since they have lowest number of credit cards and least average credit limit.

Summary and Conclusions:¶

In this project,Unsupervised machine learning methods based on Clustering were used for Credit Card Customer Segmentation for AllLife Bank. The project was to enable AllLife Bank focus on its credit card customer base in the next financial year and to improve market penetration. Specifically the project was aimed at providing suitable recommendations to the marketing team and the Operations team respectively. For the Marketing team, recommendations to enable the team to run personalised campaigns to target new customers as well as upsell to existing customers. For the Operations team recommendations to enable upgrade of the service delivery model, and to ensure that customers queries are resolved faster. In this project both the K-Means Clusstering method and the Hierachical Clustering (Agglomerative) were investigated and a number of key questions answered.
